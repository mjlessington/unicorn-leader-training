# The IT Leadership Gap

## Why Technology Organizations Need Unicorn Leaders Who Dare to Lead

---

## The Data on IT Leadership

The gap between technical competence and leadership effectiveness in IT is well-documented:

- **Only 12%** of IT professionals strongly agree that their organization does a good job developing leaders (Gallup)
- **58%** of new IT managers receive no leadership training before or after their promotion (DDI)
- **3 in 4** IT employees say their direct manager is the most stressful part of their job (Blind survey)
- **The average tenure** of a CIO is 4.3 years — the shortest of any C-suite role

These numbers tell a consistent story: the technology industry promotes for technical excellence and assumes leadership will follow. It doesn't.

## The Three Gaps

### Gap 1: The Skills Gap
Traditional IT career paths develop deep technical expertise but not the human skills that leadership demands. The result:

| Technical Skill | Leadership Skill Needed | Gap |
|----------------|------------------------|-----|
| System design | Organizational design | Leading people, not systems |
| Debugging | Conflict resolution | Diagnosing human problems |
| Code review | Coaching & feedback | Developing people, not code |
| Architecture | Strategic thinking | Business context, not just technical |
| Incident response | Crisis leadership | Calm presence under pressure |

**Vanderbloemen's framework** addresses this by naming the specific habits (builds bridges, makes others feel special, infectious energy) that are never taught in computer science programs or engineering bootcamps.

### Gap 2: The Courage Gap
Even IT leaders who have developed people skills often lack the courage to deploy them in high-stakes situations. They know they should have the tough conversation, but they send a Slack message instead. They know they should challenge the executive, but they stay silent.

**Brown's framework** addresses this directly: courage is not the absence of fear — it's the choice to show up despite it. And it's a *learnable skill set*, not a character trait.

### Gap 3: The Culture Gap
Individual leadership development fails when the organizational culture punishes the very behaviors being taught. If the organization rewards firefighting over prevention, individual contributors won't become proactive thinkers. If the culture penalizes failure, no training will make people innovative.

**Both frameworks together** address this gap: Vanderbloemen's habits define what the culture should reward, while Brown's trust and values frameworks provide the architecture for building that culture.

## What Unicorn IT Leadership Looks Like

The leader who embodies both frameworks operates differently:

### In Planning
- Names uncertainties openly (vulnerability) while taking initiative to address them (drives to the basket)
- Invites diverse perspectives (stays curious) and creates safety for disagreement (psychological safety)

### In Crisis
- Takes ownership immediately (owns the outcome) without shame or blame (rumbling with vulnerability)
- Maintains grounded energy (infectious energy) while being honest about severity (genuine authenticity)

### In Development
- Invests in people's growth as a first priority (makes others feel special) and has the hard conversations that growth requires (brave leadership)
- Builds cross-functional relationships (builds bridges) grounded in mutual trust (BRAVING)

### In Strategy
- Looks around corners (sees around corners) while staying open to being wrong (vulnerability)
- Thinks beyond their own team (thinks abundantly) and operationalizes their values in decisions (living into values)

## The Business Case

Organizations with leaders who demonstrate these combined capabilities see measurable outcomes:

- **Retention:** Teams with high psychological safety experience 50% lower turnover
- **Innovation:** Organizations where leaders model vulnerability see 3.5x more idea generation
- **Speed:** High-trust teams deliver 2x faster because they spend less time on CYA documentation and political navigation
- **Engagement:** Gallup data shows managers account for 70% of the variance in team engagement scores

The unicorn leader isn't a luxury. In a talent market where top engineers can leave for a 20% raise in 48 hours, leadership quality is a competitive advantage with direct bottom-line impact.
