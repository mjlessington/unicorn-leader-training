# Your 90-Day Action Plan

---

## Why 90 Days?

Research on behavior change shows that new habits take approximately 66 days to form (Lally et al., 2010). Ninety days gives you enough time to establish new patterns while being short enough to maintain focus and urgency.

Your 90-day plan focuses on **three commitments** — not twelve, not six, three. Brown's research shows that constraint drives follow-through. Too many goals creates the illusion of progress without actual change.

## Selecting Your Three Commitments

Review your work from all five modules. Then select three commitments — one from each category:

### Commitment 1: Personal Practice
A change in your own daily or weekly leadership behavior.

**Examples:**
- "I will start every 1:1 with 'What's on your mind?' and wait for the real answer"
- "I will name one emotion per day in a professional context"
- "I will ask 'What am I missing?' in every meeting I lead"
- "I will write down three things my team members did well each week and tell them"

### Commitment 2: Team Impact
A change you will make to how your team operates.

**Examples:**
- "I will implement blameless retrospectives for every incident"
- "I will run a BRAVING assessment with my team and address the weakest element"
- "I will create a team agreement on psychological safety norms"
- "I will redesign our interview process to assess unicorn traits"

### Commitment 3: Organizational Influence
A change you will drive beyond your immediate team.

**Examples:**
- "I will propose a cross-functional leadership development program to my VP"
- "I will start a peer coaching circle with three other IT directors"
- "I will share our team's blameless retro practice with the broader IT organization"
- "I will have the brave conversation with [specific leader] about [specific issue]"

## The Anti-Backslide Plan

Change is hard. Backsliding is normal. Plan for it in advance:

**When I backslide, I will notice because:**
(What are the early warning signs? Your armor going up? Avoiding conversations? Reverting to old habits?)

_____________________________________________

**When I notice backsliding, I will:**
(Call your accountability partner? Re-read your manifesto? Schedule a vulnerable conversation? Practice self-compassion?)

_____________________________________________

(Replace "I can't do this" or "This doesn't work" with something more accurate and generous)
