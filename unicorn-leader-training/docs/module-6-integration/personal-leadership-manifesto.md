# Your Personal Leadership Manifesto

---

## What Is a Leadership Manifesto?

A leadership manifesto is a living document that captures your leadership identity — not who you hope to be someday, but who you commit to being starting now. It draws from both frameworks:

- **Vanderbloemen's contribution:** The specific habits you will practice
- **Brown's contribution:** The values you will live, the vulnerability you will embrace, and the courage you will demonstrate

A strong manifesto is:
- **Specific** enough to guide decisions
- **Honest** enough to include your growth edges
- **Brief** enough to remember and reference
- **Personal** enough that it couldn't belong to anyone else

## Example Manifesto

> **My Leadership Manifesto**
>
> I am an IT leader who builds environments where people do the best work of their careers. I believe technology leadership is fundamentally about people, and that the most important system I design is the culture my team operates in.
>
> **My values are Candor and Growth.** When I live candor, I have difficult conversations within 48 hours. When I slip, I avoid conflict and hope problems self-resolve. When I live growth, I invest time in my team's development every week. When I slip, I prioritize delivery over development.
>
> **My unicorn strengths are:** Sees Around Corners (I anticipate strategic shifts and prepare my team), Builds Bridges (I create connections across the organization), and Genuine Authenticity (I show up as the same person in every room).
>
> **I'm developing:** Infectious Energy — I tend toward intensity rather than inspiration, and I'm working to bring more lightness and celebration to my leadership.
>
> **My courage commitment:** I will stop avoiding upward conversations. When I disagree with my VP, I will share my perspective within 24 hours, not two weeks later in a document.
>
> **My armor is intellectualization.** When emotions run high, I retreat into data, frameworks, and analysis. This protects me from vulnerability but disconnects me from my team. Instead, I commit to naming emotions in the moment: "I'm feeling uncertain about this" or "I'm concerned about what I'm seeing."
>
> **My promise to my team:** I will always tell you the truth. I will fight for your growth. I will take responsibility when I'm wrong. I will create space for you to fail safely. And I will be the same person in your 1:1 that I am in the executive meeting.
