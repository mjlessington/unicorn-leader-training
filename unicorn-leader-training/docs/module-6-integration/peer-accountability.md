# Peer Accountability

---

## Why Peer Accountability Works

Brown's research shows that accountability is most effective when it's:
- **Mutual** — both people are accountable to each other
- **Specific** — tied to concrete commitments, not vague aspirations
- **Regular** — scheduled, not ad hoc
- **Grounded in trust** — built on BRAVING, not just obligation

Individual commitment is necessary but insufficient for sustained behavior change. The research is clear: **people who have an accountability partner are 65% more likely to achieve their goals.** People who have regular accountability check-ins are 95% more likely.

## Selecting Your Accountability Partner

Your ideal accountability partner is someone who:

- **Is not your direct report or your manager** — the power dynamic interferes with honesty
- **Has completed this training** (or a similar development journey)
- **Will be genuinely honest with you** — not just encouraging, but willing to say "I don't think you're living up to your commitment"
- **You trust** — this person will hear your vulnerabilities, fears, and failures
- **Is committed to their own growth** — mutual accountability is more powerful than one-directional

## The Accountability Partnership Agreement

### Our Commitments to Each Other

1. We will meet monthly for 45-60 minutes
2. We will come prepared with honest self-assessment
3. We will practice BRAVING in every conversation
4. We will hold each other accountable with candor and compassion
5. Everything shared in these conversations stays in these conversations (Vault)
6. We will extend generous interpretation to each other (Generosity)

### The Monthly Check-In Agenda

**Opening (5 minutes):**
- How are you doing? (Real answer, not performative answer)

**Self-Assessment (15 minutes each, 30 total):**
For each of your three 90-day commitments:
1. What did I do this month toward this commitment?
2. Where did I succeed? What enabled that success?
3. Where did I struggle or backslide? What happened?
4. What's my armor telling me?
5. What do I need from my partner?

**Challenge & Support (10 minutes):**
- What's the one thing you're most avoiding right now?
- What would 10% braver look like this month?

**Commitments for Next Month (5 minutes):**
- What specifically will you do before we meet again?

### Check-In Questions for Partners

These questions go deeper than "How's it going?":

- "Tell me about a moment this month where you showed up with courage."
- "Tell me about a moment where your armor went up."
- "Are you being honest with me right now, or are you performing?"
- "What's the story you're telling yourself about your progress?"
- "What would your team say about how you've shown up this month?"
- "Where are you most tempted to quit?"

## Beyond 90 Days: Sustaining the Practice

The 90-day plan is a launch pad, not a destination. After 90 days:

### Assess and Reset
- Which commitments became habits? Celebrate and maintain.
- Which commitments stalled? Diagnose honestly — was it the wrong goal, the wrong timing, or insufficient courage?
- What new commitments emerge from 90 days of practice?

### Expand the Circle
- Invite another leader into your accountability practice
- Start a leadership development group within your organization
- Mentor someone earlier in their leadership journey using these frameworks

### Deepen the Practice
- Re-read both books with fresh eyes — you'll see things you missed
- Attend a Dare to Lead or similar facilitated program
- Teach these concepts to others (teaching is the deepest form of learning)

## The Final Reflection

Before closing this training, write your answer to this question:

**"What kind of IT leader does my team need me to be, and am I willing to do the vulnerable, courageous work to become that leader?"**
