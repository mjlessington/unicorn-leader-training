# Module 2: Self-Awareness & Authenticity

**Duration:** 90 minutes | **Format:** Self-Assessment + Reflection + Pair Work

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

1. Map their personal strengths and growth areas across both the unicorn traits and courage skill sets
2. Identify their default armor patterns and the triggers that activate them
3. Practice Brown's "rumbling with vulnerability" in a structured IT leadership context
4. Develop an emotional intelligence strategy specific to technology leadership

## Why Self-Awareness Is the Foundation

Both Vanderbloemen and Brown place self-awareness at the center of their frameworks, though they approach it from different angles:

- **Vanderbloemen** frames self-awareness through the lens of **genuine authenticity** — you can't be authentically yourself if you don't know who you are. His research shows that unicorn professionals have a clear-eyed understanding of their strengths and limitations and don't try to be someone they're not.

- **Brown** frames self-awareness as the prerequisite for every other courage skill. You can't rumble with vulnerability if you don't know what triggers your armor. You can't live into your values if you haven't identified them. You can't build trust if you don't understand your own trust patterns.

For IT leaders, self-awareness solves a specific problem: **the gap between the leader you think you are and the leader your team experiences.**

:::caution The Blind Spot Problem
Research consistently shows that leaders overestimate their self-awareness. 95% of people believe they are self-aware, but only 10-15% actually are (Eurich, 2018). IT leaders are particularly susceptible because technical competence creates a halo effect — "I'm good at my job, so I must be good at leading."
:::

## The Two Types of Self-Awareness

Organizational psychologist Tasha Eurich identifies two distinct types of self-awareness that IT leaders need:

### Internal Self-Awareness
How clearly you see your own values, passions, aspirations, fit, reactions, and impact on others.

**IT leader questions:**
- What energizes me about technology leadership? What drains me?
- When do I armor up? What triggers it?
- What are my actual values (not aspirational ones)?
- Where does my identity end and my role begin?

### External Self-Awareness
How clearly you understand how others experience you.

**IT leader questions:**
- How does my team describe my leadership style when I'm not in the room?
- What do my peers think my strengths and limitations are?
- When I think I'm being "direct," do others experience it as "harsh"?
- When I think I'm being "thoughtful," do others experience it as "indecisive"?

The research shows these two types are **independent** — you can be high on one and low on the other. The goal is to develop both.
