# Reading List & Resources

---

## Primary Texts

### Be The Unicorn: 12 Data-Driven Habits That Separate the Best Leaders from the Rest
**William Vanderbloemen** (2023)

The foundational text for understanding the 12 unicorn habits. Vanderbloemen draws from his firm's experience evaluating over 30,000 leaders to identify the specific, learnable behaviors that make certain professionals irreplaceable.

**Key chapters for IT leaders:**
- Chapter 3: "Drives to the Basket" — Initiative and agency in leadership
- Chapter 6: "Stays Curious" — The learning mindset
- Chapter 8: "Owns the Outcome" — Accountability without blame
- Chapter 12: "Builds Bridges" — Cross-functional influence

### Dare to Lead: Brave Work. Tough Conversations. Whole Hearts.
**Brene Brown** (2018)

The research-backed framework for courageous leadership. Brown translates two decades of vulnerability and shame research into practical leadership tools.

**Key chapters for IT leaders:**
- Part One: "Rumbling with Vulnerability" — The core leadership skill
- Part Two: "Living into Our Values" — Operationalizing what matters
- Part Three: "BRAVING Trust" — The anatomy of trust
- Part Four: "Learning to Rise" — Resilience after failure

## Recommended Supporting Texts

### On Self-Awareness
- **Insight** by Tasha Eurich — The definitive work on organizational self-awareness, distinguishing internal and external self-awareness
- **Emotional Intelligence** by Daniel Goleman — The foundational text on EQ in leadership

### On Psychological Safety
- **The Fearless Organization** by Amy Edmondson — The research behind psychological safety and its impact on team performance
- **The 4 Stages of Psychological Safety** by Timothy Clark — A practical framework for building safety progressively

### On Trust
- **The Speed of Trust** by Stephen M.R. Covey — Trust as an economic accelerator, with practical trust-building behaviors
- **The Five Dysfunctions of a Team** by Patrick Lencioni — Trust as the foundation of team effectiveness

### On IT Leadership Specifically
- **The Phoenix Project** by Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford — IT leadership through narrative, focusing on DevOps and organizational transformation
- **An Elegant Puzzle** by Will Larson — Systems thinking for engineering management
- **The Manager's Path** by Camille Fournier — The technical leadership career ladder
- **Team Topologies** by Matthew Skelton and Manuel Pais — Organizing teams for fast flow

### On Servant Leadership
- **Leaders Eat Last** by Simon Sinek — Why leaders who prioritize their people build stronger organizations
- **Turn the Ship Around!** by L. David Marquet — Intent-based leadership that distributes authority

### On Change Leadership
- **Switch** by Chip Heath and Dan Heath — How to change when change is hard
- **Managing Transitions** by William Bridges — The human side of organizational change

### On Culture
- **The Culture Code** by Daniel Coyle — The three skills that make groups effective
- **No Rules Rules** by Reed Hastings and Erin Meyer — Netflix's radical culture of freedom and responsibility
