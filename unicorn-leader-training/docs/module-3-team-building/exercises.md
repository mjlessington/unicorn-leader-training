## Exercise 2: Psychological Safety Audit (20 minutes)

### Part A: The Team Perspective

Answer these questions as honestly as you can about your team:

1. When was the last time someone on your team openly disagreed with you in a group setting?
   - If you can remember a recent example: what happened afterward?
   - If you can't: why not?

2. When was the last time someone told you about a mistake *before* you discovered it?
   - What was your response?
   - What message did your response send?

3. How many "surprises" have you had in the last quarter — problems you didn't know about until they were urgent?
   - Each surprise is a data point about psychological safety

### Part B: Design an Improvement

Based on your audit, design one change to your team's rituals:

| **How will you measure whether it's working?** | |
| **What does success look like in 30 days?** | |

## Exercise 3: The Hiring Redesign (25 minutes)

### For Groups

Your team has an open senior engineer position. Redesign the interview process to assess for unicorn traits alongside technical skills.

**Current typical IT interview:**
1. Phone screen (HR)
2. Technical phone screen
3. On-site: whiteboard coding
4. On-site: system design
5. On-site: "culture fit" chat with team
6. Hiring committee decision

**Redesign challenge:** Modify this process to effectively assess at least 4 unicorn traits. For each modification:
- Which trait are you assessing?
- What specific question or exercise will you use?

## Group Discussion Questions

1. **"You can't outsource trust-building to team-building events."** What does your organization spend on team building? How much of it actually builds trust versus provides entertainment?

2. **Think about the highest-performing IT team you've been part of.** Was psychological safety present? How was it created? Was it intentional or accidental?

3. **"Hiring for cultural fit can be a way to avoid the vulnerability of working with people who are different from you."** Discuss. What's the line between cultural fit and cultural comfort?

4. **Which element of BRAVING is hardest to maintain in remote/distributed IT teams?** What practices have you seen that address this?
