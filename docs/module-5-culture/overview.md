# Module 5: Culture & Organizational Impact

**Duration:** 90 minutes | **Format:** Workshop + Design Thinking + Case Analysis

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

1. Define and operationalize their leadership values using Brown's framework
2. Apply servant leadership principles through the lens of unicorn traits
3. Design cultural practices that scale unicorn behavior beyond their immediate team
4. Identify and address the cultural anti-patterns that suppress both courage and unicorn traits

## Culture Is What You Tolerate

Peter Drucker's famous line, "Culture eats strategy for breakfast," is especially true in technology organizations. But Drucker's insight leaves a critical question unanswered: **How do you build the culture you want?**

Both Vanderbloemen and Brown offer concrete answers:

- **Vanderbloemen:** Culture is built by consistently rewarding the habits you want to see. If you want unicorns, recognize and promote unicorn behavior. If you reward only technical output, you get technically excellent people who may or may not be good to work with.

- **Brown:** Culture is built by leaders who live their values visibly and who create systems that hold the organization accountable to those values. Culture isn't what you put on the wall — it's what you tolerate in the hallway.

**The combined insight:** Culture is the intersection of the habits you reward (Vanderbloemen) and the values you practice (Brown).
