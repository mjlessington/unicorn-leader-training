# Module 6: Exercises & Reflection

---

## Exercise 1: Write Your Manifesto (25 minutes)

Using the template from the previous section, write your complete Personal Leadership Manifesto. This is the most important output of the entire training.

**Guidelines:**
- Be honest, not aspirational. Write about the leader you're committed to being, not the leader you think you should describe.
- Include your growth edges. A manifesto without acknowledged limitations is a press release, not a personal document.
- Make it specific to your context. Reference your team, your organization, your industry.
- Write in first person. This is your commitment to yourself and your people.

**When you're done, read it out loud to yourself.** If it doesn't feel a little vulnerable, it's not honest enough. Revise until it is.

## Exercise 2: Build Your 90-Day Plan (20 minutes)

Select your three commitments and fill out the full plan template:

1. **Personal Practice commitment:** _____________________________________________
2. **Team Impact commitment:** _____________________________________________
3. **Organizational Influence commitment:** _____________________________________________

For each commitment, ensure you have:
- [ ] Specific, measurable definition of success
- [ ] Identified obstacles and plans to address them
- [ ] Milestone schedule with check-in dates
- [ ] Named accountability partner

---

## Exercise 3: Accountability Partner Pairing (15 minutes)

### For Group Settings

1. Stand up and find the person in this room who you believe will hold you most accountable — not the person you're most comfortable with, but the person you trust to be honest.

2. Share your three commitments with each other.

3. Schedule your first monthly check-in. Put it in your calendar right now.

4. Exchange the contact information you'll use (not just email — the channel you'll actually use).

5. Each person answers this question to their partner: "The thing I'm most likely to let slide is ___________. When I do, I need you to ___________."

### For Individual Learners

1. Identify 2-3 potential accountability partners from your professional network.

2. Reach out to your top choice with a specific ask: "I've been working through a leadership development program, and I'd like to propose a monthly accountability partnership. Here's what it would involve..."

3. If your first choice declines, move to the next. Don't settle for someone who won't challenge you.

---

## Exercise 4: The Letter to Yourself (10 minutes)

Write a letter to yourself, to be opened in 90 days. Include:

- What you learned in this training that surprised you
- What you're most excited about
- What you're most afraid of
- Your three commitments, in your own words
- A message of encouragement from your current self to your future self who might be struggling

Seal the letter (or schedule an email to yourself for 90 days from today). Open it when your 90-day plan concludes.

---

## Closing Circle (For Group Settings)

Each participant shares one sentence:

**"The one thing I'm taking from this training and putting into practice starting tomorrow is ___________."**

No explanations. No caveats. No "I'll try to." Just one specific commitment.
