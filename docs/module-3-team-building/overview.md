# Module 3: Building Unicorn Teams

**Duration:** 90 minutes | **Format:** Framework Study + Case Analysis + Group Design

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

1. Apply Vanderbloemen's unicorn traits as a hiring and development framework
2. Implement Google's psychological safety model enhanced by Brown's trust framework
3. Use the BRAVING inventory to diagnose and repair trust on their teams
4. Design team rituals that reinforce unicorn habits and courageous behavior

## From Individual to Team

Modules 1 and 2 focused on you — the individual leader. This module shifts the lens outward: **how do you build a team of unicorns, and how do you create the conditions where unicorn behavior is possible?**

The critical insight from both frameworks: **you cannot hire your way to a unicorn team.** You can hire for unicorn *potential*, but whether that potential activates or atrophies depends entirely on the environment you create.

Brown puts it directly: *"A brave leader is not someone who is born brave. Brave leadership is a collection of skills that are 100% teachable, observable, and measurable. But they require a culture that supports and rewards courage."*

Vanderbloemen agrees: *"Unicorn habits emerge in organizations that recognize and reward them. In organizations that punish initiative, penalize failure, and hoard information, even natural unicorns go dormant."*

## The Team Unicorn Model

Building a unicorn team requires three layers:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│     3. TEAM PRACTICES               │
│     Rituals, norms, and habits      │
│     that reinforce unicorn behavior │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│     2. PSYCHOLOGICAL SAFETY         │
│     The environment that makes      │
│     courage possible                │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│     1. TRUST INFRASTRUCTURE         │
│     The BRAVING framework that      │
│     creates the foundation          │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

You build from the bottom up. Without trust, there's no safety. Without safety, there's no courage. Without courage, there are no unicorns.
