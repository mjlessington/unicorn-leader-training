# Module 1: Exercises & Reflection

---

## Exercise 1: Your Leadership Origin Story (15 minutes)

Write a brief narrative (1-2 paragraphs) answering these questions:

1. **Who was the best leader you've ever worked for in technology?** What made them exceptional?
2. **Which of the 12 unicorn traits did they demonstrate most strongly?**
3. **How did they handle vulnerability?** Did they armor up or show up?

:::tip For Group Settings
Share your story with a partner. Listen for patterns: which traits come up most frequently? Which are conspicuously absent? What does this tell us about what IT professionals value in leaders versus what they typically receive?
:::

## Exercise 2: Armor Inventory (20 minutes)

Review the six common armor patterns from the vulnerability section. For each one, rate how frequently you use it on a scale of 1 (rarely) to 5 (frequently):

| Armor Pattern | Frequency (1-5) | Specific Situation Where You Use It |

| Cynicism / Been-there-done-that | | |

**Reflection questions:**
- What is your highest-rated armor? What are you protecting yourself from when you use it?

## Exercise 3: The Unicorn-Courage Matrix (20 minutes)

For each of your top 3 unicorn traits and bottom 3 unicorn traits, identify the vulnerability required to demonstrate them:

**Example:**

| Unicorn Trait | My Rating | Vulnerability Required | My Current Armor |
|--------------|:---------:|----------------------|-----------------|
| Drives to the basket | 4 | Risk of being wrong or overstepping | Sometimes wait for permission when stakes are high |
| Genuine authenticity | 2 | Risk of being judged for not knowing | Default to "executive voice" in senior meetings |

Complete the matrix for your own top 3 and bottom 3 traits.

## Group Discussion Questions (For Facilitated Sessions)

1. **"Tech culture celebrates 'fail fast' but punishes actual failure."** Do you agree? How does this paradox affect vulnerability in your organization?

2. **Which unicorn trait is most valued in your organization? Which is most rare?** What does the gap tell you?

3. **Think about the last major IT incident or project failure in your org.** How did leadership respond? Was there armoring? What would a unicorn/daring leader response have looked like?

4. **"You can't be a unicorn and be armored."** Discuss this statement. Is it true? Are there exceptions?
