# Facilitator Guide

---

## Overview

This guide provides everything you need to facilitate *The Unicorn Leader* training for groups of 8-20 IT leaders. The training can be delivered in three formats:

| Format | Duration | Best For |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Intensive** | 2 days (9 hours + breaks) | Leadership retreats, dedicated development time |
| **Series** | 6 sessions x 90 minutes | Weekly or bi-weekly cohort programs |
| **Hybrid** | Pre-work + 1 day in person | Teams with limited in-person time |

## Facilitator Prerequisites

Before facilitating this training, you should:

- [ ] Read both *Be The Unicorn* and *Dare to Lead* completely
- [ ] Complete all six modules yourself, including exercises
- [ ] Write your own Personal Leadership Manifesto
- [ ] Have facilitation experience with leadership development content
- [ ] Be comfortable with vulnerability in group settings (you will model it)

## Room Setup and Materials

### Physical Space
- Tables for groups of 4-5 (not classroom-style rows)
- Whiteboard or flip chart with markers
- Wall space for posting group work
- Comfortable seating — people will be here for extended periods

### Materials Per Participant
- Printed workbook (all exercises from each module)
- Pen and notepad
- Sticky notes (2 pads per person)
- Access to the training site (for reference and pre/post-work)

### Technology
- Projector for slides and site display
- Timer visible to all participants
- Wi-Fi for site access during exercises

## Detailed Session Plans

### Module 1: Foundations (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Format | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Welcome and ground rules | Full group | Set the tone: this is a space for honesty and growth |
| 0:05-0:20 | The IT Leadership Gap | Presentation | Use the data from the module. Let it land. |
| 0:20-0:35 | The 12 Unicorn Traits | Interactive presentation | Show each trait with IT examples. Ask: "Who resonates with this one?" |
| 0:35-0:50 | Vulnerability as Strength | Presentation + discussion | Present the six myths. Ask participants to identify which myths they carry. |
| 0:50-1:05 | Exercise: Unicorn Self-Assessment | Individual | Quiet time. Provide the rating sheet. |
| 1:05-1:20 | Exercise: Leadership Origin Story | Pairs | 5 minutes each. Find the patterns. |
| 1:20-1:30 | Full group debrief + key takeaways | Discussion | Ask: "What surprised you?" |

**Facilitator tip:** Module 1 sets the emotional tone. If participants feel safe here, they'll go deeper in later modules. Don't rush through the vulnerability section — it needs time to land.

### Module 2: Self-Awareness (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Format | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Check-in: One word for how you're showing up today | Round-robin | Models self-awareness from the start |
| 0:05-0:20 | Internal vs. External Self-Awareness | Presentation | The Eurich research. The 95% vs. 15% statistic always lands. |
| 0:20-0:40 | Values Identification Exercise | Individual | This is hard. Give them time. Don't rush the narrowing process. |
| 0:40-0:55 | Values Sharing | Pairs or triads | Share values and behaviors. Partners offer feedback: "I've seen you live that value when..." |
| 0:55-1:10 | Rumbling with Vulnerability | Presentation + demo | Model a rumble. Use a real example from your own leadership. |
| 1:10-1:25 | Rumble Practice | Pairs | Use the scenarios from the exercises. Give clear role-play structure. |
| 1:25-1:30 | Debrief + key takeaways | Discussion | Ask: "What was hard about the rumble practice?" |

**Facilitator tip:** The values exercise will feel uncomfortable — people resist narrowing to two. That discomfort is the point. Don't rescue them from it.

### Module 3: Team Building (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Format | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Check-in | Round-robin | |
| 0:05-0:20 | Psychological Safety overview | Presentation | The four stages. Google's Project Aristotle data. |
| 0:20-0:35 | BRAVING Trust deep dive | Interactive | Walk through each element with IT examples. |
| 0:35-0:55 | Team BRAVING Assessment | Individual + pairs | Rate your own team, then share with a partner. |
| 0:55-1:15 | Hiring Redesign Exercise | Small groups | Groups redesign an interview process. Present back. |
| 1:15-1:25 | Trust Repair Scenario | Full group discussion | Use the scenario from the exercises. |
| 1:25-1:30 | Key takeaways | Individual | Write three takeaways silently. |

**Facilitator tip:** The BRAVING assessment often surfaces emotions. People realize where they've been eroding trust. Hold space for this without trying to fix it.

### Module 4: Courageous Leadership (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Format | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Check-in | Round-robin | |
| 0:05-0:15 | Brave Conversations framework | Presentation | The script structures. "Clear is kind." |
| 0:15-0:30 | Avoided Conversation Audit | Individual | This is private. Don't ask people to share unless they volunteer. |
| 0:30-0:50 | Brave Conversation Role Play | Pairs | Each person practices their #1 avoided conversation opening. |
| 0:50-1:05 | Leading Through Change | Presentation + discussion | Day Two concept. The emotional journey of change. |
| 1:05-1:20 | Innovation and Failure discussion | Full group | Use the case study. Ask: "What would your organization have done?" |
| 1:20-1:30 | Courage Budget exercise + takeaways | Individual | |

**Facilitator tip:** The role play is the highest-vulnerability exercise in the training. Some people will resist. Don't force it, but don't let the group opt out either. Model it first with a brave volunteer.

### Module 5: Culture (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Format | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Check-in | Round-robin | |
| 0:05-0:20 | Values-Driven Culture | Presentation | The gap between stated and practiced values. Anti-patterns. |
| 0:20-0:40 | Values Operationalization Workshop | Small groups | Groups identify values and define behaviors. Present back. |
| 0:40-0:55 | Servant Leadership | Presentation + discussion | The five practices. "Lead from behind." |

| 1:25-1:30 | Key takeaways | Individual | |

**Facilitator tip:** The anti-pattern discussion can become a venting session. Redirect toward agency: "Now that you've named it, what will you do about it?"

### Module 6: Integration (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Format | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Check-in: one word for where you are now vs. where you started | Round-robin | |
| 0:05-0:30 | Write Your Manifesto | Individual | Quiet, focused time. Play ambient music if appropriate. |
| 0:30-0:40 | Manifesto Sharing | Pairs | Each person reads their manifesto aloud to a partner. |
| 0:40-0:55 | 90-Day Action Plan | Individual | Select three commitments. Fill out the template. |
| 0:55-1:10 | Accountability Partner Pairing | Pairs | Find a partner. Share commitments. Schedule the first check-in. |
| 1:10-1:20 | Letter to Yourself | Individual | Seal it. Schedule the opening date. |
| 1:20-1:30 | Closing Circle | Full group | One sentence from each person: "Starting tomorrow, I will..." |

**Facilitator tip:** The closing circle is powerful. Keep it tight — one sentence per person, no elaboration. The constraint forces clarity and creates momentum.

## Handling Difficult Moments

### Resistance ("This is soft stuff")
Don't argue. Ask: "What would change in your organization if every IT leader practiced these skills? What's the business impact?" Let the group answer.

### Emotional moments
Hold space. Don't rush past them. A brief "Thank you for sharing that — that takes courage" is sufficient. Don't therapize.

### The cynic
Engage with curiosity, not defensiveness: "It sounds like you've had experiences that make this feel unrealistic. Can you say more about that?" Often, the cynic has been burned by previous leadership development that wasn't followed through.

### Uneven participation
Use structured turn-taking (round-robin, think-pair-share) to ensure quieter participants have space. Don't cold-call in vulnerability exercises.

## Post-Training Follow-Up

### Week 1
- Send a summary email with links to the training site
- Remind participants to schedule their first accountability check-in

### Month 1
- Send a brief pulse survey: "Have you had your first accountability check-in? What's working? What's challenging?"
- Share any resources or articles that connect to training themes

### Month 3
- Facilitate a virtual reunion session (60 minutes)
- Each participant shares progress on their 90-day plan
- Facilitate a group discussion on what they've learned from practice

### Month 6
- Assess organizational impact: Has anything changed in the team/org culture?
- Consider offering an "advanced" session for participants who want to go deeper
