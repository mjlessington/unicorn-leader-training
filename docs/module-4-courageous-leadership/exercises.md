## Exercise 2: Change Leadership Simulation (30 minutes)

### For Groups

**Scenario:** Your CIO has decided to consolidate three separate IT teams (Application Development, Infrastructure, and Data) into a single "Digital Solutions" team. You are the newly appointed director of this combined team. You have 45 people who didn't ask for this change, three former team leads who just lost their director titles, and a 90-day mandate to "be fully operational."

### Part A: Plan Your Approach (15 minutes)

Using both frameworks, design your first 30 days:

**Week 1: Trust Foundation**
- What will your first message to the combined team contain? Draft the key points.
- How will you apply BRAVING in your first interactions?
- Which unicorn traits are most critical in week 1?

**Week 2: Listening & Learning**
- What questions will you ask in your 1:1s with each of the 45 team members?
- How will you address the three former directors' loss of title and autonomy?
- Where will you be vulnerable? Where will you be clear?

**Weeks 3-4: Direction Setting**
- How will you co-create the new team's identity without dismissing the old ones?
- What rituals will you establish to build the new culture?
- How will you handle the inevitable "this is stupid" resistance?

### Part B: The Hard Moment (15 minutes)

Role-play or discuss: It's day 15. One of the former directors comes to your 1:1 and says:

*"I've been a director for four years. I built that team from scratch. And now I'm a 'team lead' reporting to someone who doesn't understand our domain. I have a recruiter reaching out to me and honestly, I'm listening."*

Practice your response. Use Brown's techniques (curiosity, vulnerability, "the story I'm telling myself") and Vanderbloemen's traits (genuine authenticity, makes others feel special, owns the outcome).

## Exercise 3: Innovation Failure Post-Mortem (15 minutes)

Think about the most significant failure or setback in your IT career. Work through this framework:

### The Reckoning
- What happened? (Facts only — no judgment, no interpretation)
- What emotion did you experience? (Name it specifically)
- What was the story you told yourself about it?

### The Rumble
- What was actually true versus what you assumed?
- What role did you play in the outcome?
- What did you learn that you couldn't have learned any other way?
- How did this failure shape your leadership?

### The Revolution
- How has this experience changed how you lead?
- What would you tell another IT leader going through the same thing?
- If you could go back, what would you do the same? What would you change?

## Group Discussion Questions

1. **"In IT, we say 'fail fast' but we don't say 'fail safely.'"** What's the difference? How do you create conditions for safe failure?

2. **Think about a time you witnessed courageous leadership in a technology organization.** What happened? What made it courageous? What was the outcome?

3. **"The conversations we avoid define the culture we create."** What conversations is your organization systematically avoiding? What culture is that creating?

4. **Brown says "clear is kind, unclear is unkind."** Where in your IT leadership are you being unclear because you think you're being kind?
